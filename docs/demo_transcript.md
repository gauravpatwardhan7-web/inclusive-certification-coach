## Full transcript

**Overview & landing page.** This is the Inclusive Certification Coach — an
accessibility-first learning coach for enterprise teams. It's built as eight
cooperating agents, grounded in Microsoft Foundry IQ, with every claim cited.
The clean landing page invites the learner to set a goal and build a plan.

**Three tabs and the sidebar.** Three tabs sit at the top: Learn, Manager view,
and How it works. In the sidebar I pick my certification — AZ-204 — my role,
and how I work best. I note ADHD and trouble focusing, then choose Build my plan.

**A grounded, cited learning path.** The Curator retrieves grounded content and
builds a cited learning path. Each module shows recommended hours, an
accommodation note, and its source citation. Behind it, the Study Plan Generator
schedules short, focus-friendly blocks with breaks. Every step is grounded in
retrieved knowledge-base chunks — no hallucinated facts.

**Listen mode.** With Listen mode on, the Accessibility Narrator turns the
result into a spoken script. There's a real player — play, pause, choose a
voice, and adjust the speed. Symbols and abbreviations are read as words, so
it's screen-reader friendly.

**Calendar Negotiator.** Next, the Calendar Negotiator fits study into my real
week. It finds genuine gaps between meetings and books study blocks into them.
I can adjust the daily cap, block length, and preferred time — and it re-books
instantly. If the week is too full, it pushes back with evidence instead of
pretending it fits.

**Check your readiness.** Now the readiness check — and I choose how I'm tested.
I can pick exactly which topics to be quizzed on, and how many questions. The
questions are realistic, exam-style scenarios — not trivia about the guide. Or I
can switch to Teach-it-back and explain a concept in my own words. It grades my
understanding, names what I covered, and flags what's missing, then asks one
Socratic follow-up aimed at the biggest gap. I answer the quick questions and
submit them.

**The Orchestrator's decision & focused loop.** The Assessment Agent scores me
and reports my weak areas. Then the Orchestrator reasons over my score, weak
areas, and attempt history. It decides one of three actions: advance, loop back,
or escalate to a human. Here it loops me back — to study only the areas I got
wrong. One click starts a focused refresher, re-quizzing just those topics. The
loop is real reasoning, not a fixed script.

**Advocate — a note to your manager.** If I need support, the Advocate drafts a
note to my manager for me. I choose whether to share my accessibility profile —
it's private by default. Redaction is enforced in code, so the model can't leak
what it never saw. The draft shows exactly what it shares and what it keeps
private. Nothing is sent without my approval.

**Manager view.** The Manager view rolls the whole team up for a people manager.
Manager Insights reasons over each learner's progress against the readiness
threshold. Profiles of learners who haven't opted in stay redacted here too.

**How it works.** Finally, How it works makes the reasoning legible. It logs
every agent step, the chunks it retrieved, and their relevance scores, and it
shows the orchestrator's signals weighed and alternatives rejected. Deterministic
guardrails keep it safe — stalled learners always reach a human. Grounded,
reliable, and accessibility-first — backed by 72 of 72 eval checks. Thanks for
watching.
